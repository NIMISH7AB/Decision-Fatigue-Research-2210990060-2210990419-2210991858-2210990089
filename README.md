## Project Description
This research study investigates the impact of digital connectivity and after-hours engagement on decision fatigue and perceived decision quality among corporate managers. The study applies empirical statistical techniques such as descriptive analysis, Pearson correlation, and multiple regression analysis to identify relationships between cognitive overload, digital connectivity intensity, and managerial decision-making performance.

## Key Findings
- Higher digital connectivity significantly increases decision fatigue.
- After-hours digital engagement contributes to cognitive exhaustion.
- Decision fatigue negatively affects perceived decision quality.
- Correlation and regression analysis validate the proposed research hypotheses.
